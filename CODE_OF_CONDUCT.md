# Code of Conduct

## Our pledge

Concordance is built for and used by students, teachers, and schools. In the interest of
fostering an open and welcoming environment, contributors and maintainers pledge to make
participation in this project a harassment-free experience for everyone, regardless of age,
body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience,
nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

Because this is an education community, we hold a higher bar: assume good faith, be patient
with people who are learning, and keep discussion constructive.

## Our standards

Examples of behavior that contributes to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language.
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences.
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism.
- Focusing on what is best for learners and the community.

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Harassment, insults, or derogatory comments, and personal or political attacks.
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission.
- Sexualized language or imagery, or unwelcome sexual attention. Given that minors may be
  present in this community, this is treated with zero tolerance.
- Other conduct which could reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional or
  educational setting.

## Enforcement

Instances of abusive, harassing, or otherwise unacceptable behavior may be reported
privately through the repository's
**[Security / private reporting tab](https://github.com/monsieur-trenton/concordance/security)**
or by contacting the maintainer **[@monsieur-trenton](https://github.com/monsieur-trenton)**
on GitHub. All complaints will be reviewed and investigated, and will result in a response
appropriate to the circumstances. Maintainers may remove, edit, or reject contributions,
comments, and issues that violate this Code of Conduct, and may ban temporarily or
permanently any contributor for behaviors they deem inappropriate.
